# Pet Nutrition Guide

## Foods That Are Toxic to Pets

### Toxic to Dogs
- **Grapes and raisins**: Can cause acute kidney failure — even a small amount is dangerous.
- **Xylitol** (artificial sweetener in gum, candy, peanut butter, baked goods): Causes dangerous blood sugar drop and liver failure.
- **Chocolate**: Theobromine causes vomiting, seizures, and can be fatal. Dark chocolate and baking chocolate are the most dangerous.
- **Onions, garlic, leeks, chives**: Damage red blood cells and cause anemia. Cooked or raw — both are toxic.
- **Macadamia nuts**: Cause weakness, tremors, and fever.
- **Avocado**: Persin in the flesh and skin can cause vomiting and breathing difficulty.
- **Alcohol**: Even small amounts cause central nervous system depression.
- **Caffeine**: Coffee, tea, energy drinks — causes tremors and heart arrhythmia.
- **Cooked bones**: Can splinter and cause internal punctures. Raw bones are safer but still carry risks.

### Toxic to Cats
- **Onions, garlic, and chives**: Destroy red blood cells.
- **Grapes and raisins**: Kidney damage (extent of toxicity still being studied, but avoid).
- **Alcohol and caffeine**: Same risks as for dogs.
- **Xylitol**: Highly toxic.
- **Dog food as a long-term diet**: Lacks taurine which cats require — leads to heart disease (dilated cardiomyopathy) and blindness.
- **Raw fish in excess**: Contains thiaminase which destroys thiamine (vitamin B1), causing neurological problems.
- **Milk and dairy**: Most adult cats are lactose intolerant; causes digestive upset.
- **Chocolate**: Toxic due to theobromine, same as dogs.

### Toxic to Rabbits
- **Onions, garlic, leeks**: Cause blood disorders.
- **Avocado**: Highly toxic — causes breathing difficulty and death.
- **Rhubarb**: Causes urinary problems and can be fatal.
- **Iceberg lettuce**: Contains lactucarium which is mildly sedative; too watery and provides little nutrition.
- **Potatoes and corn**: High starch disrupts digestive bacteria and causes GI problems.
- **Processed human food**: Bread, crackers, pasta — cause dangerous GI imbalances.
- **Nuts and seeds**: Too high in fat.

## Healthy Treat Options

### Safe Dog Treats
- Baby carrots, apple slices (no seeds), blueberries, watermelon (no seeds or rind), cooked plain chicken, cucumber, green beans, cooked plain sweet potato.

### Safe Cat Treats
- Cooked plain chicken, salmon, tuna (in moderation, not as a staple), cooked egg, plain cooked shrimp. Treats should be no more than 10% of daily caloric intake.

### Safe Rabbit Treats
- Apple (without seeds), blueberries, strawberries, papaya, pineapple — only 1–2 tablespoons per day. Treats should be rare because of high sugar content.

## Portion Guidelines

### Dogs
- Use the feeding guide on your specific dog food package as a starting point.
- Adjust based on the dog's body condition score — you should be able to feel ribs but not see them.
- Treats and extras should not exceed 10% of daily caloric intake.
- Dogs prone to weight gain (Labrador, Beagle, Cocker Spaniel) need strict portion control.

### Cats
- An average 4 kg adult cat needs roughly 200–250 calories per day.
- Wet food: approximately 85g cans, 2 per day for most adult cats.
- Dry food: roughly ¼ cup per meal for an average cat — but check the specific brand's guidelines.
- Monitor weight monthly; obesity in cats leads to diabetes, arthritis, and liver disease.

### Rabbits
- Unlimited timothy hay (this is not optional — it is essential for dental and gut health).
- 1 cup of fresh leafy greens per 1 kg of body weight per day.
- Pellets: ¼ cup per 2 kg of body weight per day — measured, not free-fed.

## Water Intake

- Fresh, clean water should always be available for every pet.
- Cats naturally have a low thirst drive — wet food significantly helps with hydration.
- Dogs need approximately 30 ml of water per kilogram of body weight per day in normal conditions; more in heat or after exercise.
- Dehydration signs: sunken eyes, dry gums, skin that doesn't spring back when gently pinched.

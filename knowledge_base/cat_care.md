# Cat Care Guide

## Exercise and Play

Cats need daily play and physical activity even if they appear independent and sleep a lot.

- Indoor cats especially need stimulation since they lack natural hunting opportunities.
- Aim for two 10–15 minute interactive play sessions per day using wand toys, laser pointers, or feather toys.
- Kittens (under 1 year) need 30–45 minutes of active play per day split into short bursts.
- Senior cats (10+) still benefit from gentle daily play; shorter sessions of 5–10 minutes.
- Cats are crepuscular — most active at dawn and dusk — schedule play around those times for best engagement.
- Scratching posts, cat trees, and window perches provide physical and mental stimulation passively.

## Feeding Schedule

- **Adult cats**: 2–3 measured meals per day is ideal; avoid free feeding dry food all day as it often leads to obesity.
- **Kittens**: 3–4 small meals per day to support rapid growth.
- **Senior cats (10+)**: 2 smaller meals with senior-formula food; monitor for weight changes.
- Cats are obligate carnivores — they need animal protein to thrive. High-protein, low-carbohydrate food is best.
- Wet food helps with hydration since cats have a low thirst drive.
- Always have fresh water available; many cats prefer a fountain over a still bowl.

## Grooming

- **Short-haired cats**: Brush once a week to reduce shedding and hairballs.
- **Long-haired cats** (Persian, Maine Coon): Brush daily or every other day to prevent painful mats.
- Cats self-groom extensively but still benefit from occasional baths if they get dirty (very infrequently).
- Trim nails every 2–3 weeks using cat nail clippers.
- Check ears weekly; clean gently with a vet-approved solution if you see wax buildup.
- Dental care: brush teeth 3 times per week or provide dental treats/water additives.

## Litter Box

- Rule of thumb: one litter box per cat, plus one extra (e.g., 2 cats = 3 boxes).
- Scoop at least once daily; full clean and litter replacement every 1–2 weeks.
- Cats often reject boxes that are too dirty — this is a common cause of inappropriate elimination.
- Provide boxes in quiet, accessible locations away from food and water bowls.
- Covered vs. uncovered is a personal preference for each cat — watch which they use most.

## Enrichment and Indoor Environment

- Vertical space (cat trees, wall shelves) is critical — cats feel safe when elevated.
- Provide at least one scratching post per room where the cat spends time.
- Window access for bird-watching is mentally stimulating and keeps cats calm.
- Rotate toys regularly to prevent boredom; novelty matters more than quantity.

## Veterinary Care

- Annual wellness exam for adult cats; every 6 months for seniors.
- Core vaccines: feline distemper (FVRCP) and rabies.
- Keep up with flea/parasite prevention even for indoor cats.
- Spay/neuter by 5–6 months old.

# Dog Care Guide

## Exercise Requirements

Dogs need regular daily exercise to stay healthy and happy. The amount depends on breed, age, and health.

- **Small breeds** (Chihuahua, Shih Tzu, Pomeranian): 20–30 minutes of walking per day, split into 1–2 walks.
- **Medium breeds** (Beagle, Cocker Spaniel, Border Collie): 45–60 minutes per day, ideally two walks.
- **Large breeds** (Labrador, Golden Retriever, German Shepherd): 60–90 minutes per day across two or three walks.
- **High-energy breeds** (Border Collie, Husky, Dalmatian): Up to 2 hours per day including off-leash running or play.
- **Senior dogs (7+ years)**: Shorter, gentler walks — 15–20 minutes, 2–3 times per day is usually enough.
- **Puppies under 6 months**: 5 minutes per month of age, twice a day (e.g., 3-month-old gets 15 min twice).

Signs your dog needs more exercise: destructive chewing, excessive barking, hyperactivity indoors, weight gain.

## Feeding Schedule

Dogs generally do best with structured mealtimes rather than free feeding.

- **Adult dogs**: 2 meals per day — morning and evening — works well for most breeds.
- **Puppies (under 6 months)**: 3–4 small meals spread through the day to support growth.
- **Senior dogs**: 2 smaller meals per day; senior-formula food helps with joint and digestive health.
- Avoid feeding immediately before or after vigorous exercise to reduce bloat risk.
- Always provide fresh water throughout the day.
- Measure portions based on body weight and the food manufacturer's guidelines to prevent obesity.

## Grooming

- **Short-coated breeds** (Beagle, Boxer): Brush once weekly; bathe every 4–6 weeks.
- **Long-coated breeds** (Golden Retriever, Collie): Brush 3–4 times per week to prevent matting; bathe every 4–6 weeks.
- **Double-coated breeds** (Husky, Akita): Brush 2–3 times per week; daily brushing during shedding season.
- Trim nails every 3–4 weeks or when you hear clicking on hard floors.
- Check and clean ears weekly to prevent infections, especially in floppy-eared breeds.
- Brush teeth ideally daily, or at minimum 3 times per week, using dog-safe toothpaste.

## Socialization and Mental Stimulation

- Dogs are social animals and need regular interaction with people and other dogs.
- Aim for at least one training session per day (10–15 minutes) to keep the mind sharp.
- Puzzle feeders, sniff walks, and hide-and-seek games provide mental enrichment.
- A bored dog is more likely to develop destructive or anxious behaviors.
- Puppies should be exposed to different people, animals, and environments between 3–16 weeks (socialization window).

## Sleep and Rest

- Adult dogs sleep 12–14 hours per day on average.
- Puppies and senior dogs sleep more — up to 18–20 hours.
- Provide a comfortable, quiet sleeping area away from drafts and foot traffic.

## Veterinary Care

- Annual wellness exams for adult dogs; twice yearly for seniors (7+).
- Core vaccinations (rabies, distemper, parvovirus) on schedule from your vet.
- Monthly heartworm prevention and regular flea/tick control.
- Spay or neuter unless breeding — reduces cancer risk and behavioral issues.
